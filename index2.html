Section 1/5

1) What Is Data & Analytics?

Definition

Collecting, cleaning, and transforming data to discover patterns, answer questions, and support decisions with evidence.

Role in Organizations

  • Expose trends & opportunities for growth
  • Support strategy and day‑to‑day operations
  • Measure outcomes and drive accountability

Daily Activities

  • Gather & validate data from systems
  • Clean, join, and analyze datasets
  • Build dashboards & explain insights to stakeholders

2) Career Exploration

Entry‑Level

  • Data Analyst — reports, dashboards, stakeholder support
  • BI Analyst — visualization (Power BI/Tableau), KPIs

Mid‑Level

  • Data Engineer — pipelines, quality, scalable data
  • ML Engineer — predictive models & automation

Senior

  • Data Scientist — advanced stats/ML aligned to strategy
  • Business Analyst — requirements, process, decision support

Core Skills & Certifications

  • Skills: SQL, Python/R, Excel, Power BI/Tableau, communication
  • Certs: Google Data Analytics, AWS/Azure Data, Microsoft Power BI, ICCP

3) Professional Interviews: Voices from 5 Business Analysts

Career Journeys

“I started my career in operations and because I had deep expertise in operations I was asked to develop new products and reports. Eventually that became a full time thing and I made a career switch from Operations to Product and now to Business Analyst.” — Haik K.
“Job stability/security, decent pay, and recognizing the importance of systems.” — Melvie O.
“I originally started as a software trainer … Since then, I've worked as a software product analyst, business analyst, and data manager for a variety of companies.” — Nate M.
“I like speaking with people and understanding their work processes … BA was a role that fit my strengths.” — Jaspal S.

Day‑to‑Day Work

“Tactically, helping customers troubleshoot … Strategically, collecting data from stakeholders … then helping implementation.” — Haik K.
“Checking emails, tickets, prioritizing escalations, doing testing, running reports, documenting processes …” — Melvie O.
“The classic answer is: it depends on the day … writing requirements, documenting processes, conducting testing …” — Nate M.
“A BA role should be heavy on projects … in higher ed I have a lot of tickets from faculty and students.” — Jaspal S.

What They Enjoy

“I enjoy my job because I get to act as a bridge between people and technology …” — Haik K.
“Think outside the box … help others and improve processes.” — Melvie O.
“I enjoy being the liaison between end users and technical staff …” — Nate M.
“Solving problems with creative solutions with great people in ITS.” — Jaspal S.

Advice to Students

“Find an industry you're passionate about and apply your data skills there …” — Haik K.
“Sometimes experience matters more than education … get exposure in a job that helps your goal.” — Melvie O.
“Technical background is important, but so is communicating with non‑technical audiences …” — Nate M.
“Be solid with technical skills and invest in communication — listen and interpret.” — Jaspal S.
“Start with SQL and a BI tool like Power BI or Tableau to build a foundation.” — Carl Y.

5) Conclusion & Key Takeaways

  • Data & Analytics powers decisions in every industry.
  • Multiple paths: technical, analytical, and business‑facing.
  • From our BA survey: communication + SQL + systems context matter most.
  • Start building now: a SQL query, a dashboard, and a short write‑up.
